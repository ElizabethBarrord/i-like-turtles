FAQ

What is the difference between a tortoise and a turtle?

The main difference is that tortoises live entirely on land, whereas turtles live mostly in the ocean, but there are other differences as well. Notably, tortoises have bulkier and heavier shells, and usually live much longer than turtles.

Why is a painted turtle called a painted turtle?

Good question. As you can see here, painted turtles have long stripes running across their body in very distinct patterns, almost like someone painted them on; these are what give the painted turtle its name.

How much do you like turtles?

We don't like, we love turtles! We have stories upon stories of turtles! That's why we made this page all about turtles.

What does a pet turtle need to be happy?

Much of what your turtle will need is going to depend on its species, so make sure you are familiar with its breed and diet; you should also try to tailor certain aspects of its habitat according to that species's preferences as well. More generally, they will need a large terrarium with both water and land inside; make sure that the water is cleaned and changed regularly, and that both the air and water temperature are kept at what a wild turtle of your pet's species would find. Finally, natural sunlight helps your turtle avoid certain health problems, so placement of the terrarium should be considered.

Are turtles actually slow, or is that just a false misconception?

The world record for the fastest tortoise is .92 feet per second. There is no known record for the world's fastest turtle.
